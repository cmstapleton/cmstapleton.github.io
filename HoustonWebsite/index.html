Houston is the largest city in Texas and the fourth largest in the United States. If planning a trip to this great city, three amazing places to visit are the Johnson Space Center, the Houston Museum of Natural Science and Rice University


Johnson Space Centre

The Johnson Space Center is an interactive museum that teaches visitors about history of space and space travel, it also includes a full scale replica of a space shuttle that you can walk into. At the Johnson Space Center you will also have the unique opportunity to take a tour through a live NASA facility and visit rocket park, which houses one of only two remaining Saturn V Rockets.

Rocket Park

At NASA's Johnson Space Center you will have the opportunity to go on a 2 hour guided tour around a live NASA facility and Rocket Park. You will see the Smokey Joe and Mercury Redstone rockets plus the H-1, F-1 and J-2 rocket engines on the outside. Then you will enjoy an up close walk around the massive Saturn V moon rocket inside the main building.


Houston Museum of Natural Science

As one of the most heavily attended museums in the United States, the Museum houses the Burke Baker Planetarium, Wortham GIANT Screen Theatre, Cockrell Butterfly Center and a fascinating variety of permanent exhibit areas that examine astronomy, space science, paleontology, energy, chemistry and much more. The Museum is a major science learning center with over a half million school children visiting annually, including every fourth grade and seventh grade class in the Houston Independent School District.

Rice University

Rice University is a comprehensive research university located on a beautiful campus in Houston, Texas. Rice produces the next generation of leaders and advances tomorrow’s thinking. Located in an urban environment on a 300-acre tree-lined campus, Rice University seizes its advantageous position to pursue pathbreaking research and create innovative collaboration opportunities that contribute to the betterment of our world.
